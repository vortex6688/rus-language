Greeting in Russian

English:
Official greeting / Оффициальное приветствие:
1) Hello (+ i wish you to be healthy)
2) Hello (+ i wish you to be healthy) - full/extended version
3) Good Morning
4) Good Afternoon / Good day
5) Good Evening

Unofficial greeting / Неффициальное приветствие:
1) Hi / Hello / Hey / Yo + How are you? / How are you doing? / How are things? / How is it going?
2) Hi / Hello / Hey / Yo + What's new? / What's up? / What's going on?
3) Hi / Hello / Hey / Yo


Russian:
Official greeting / Оффициальное приветствие:
1) Здравствуйте (zdravstvuyte - plural/respectful) - May be used in any case
   Здравствуй (zdravstvuy - singular) - Mostly used when greeting a friend / single person
2) Здравия Желаю (Zdravia Zhelayu) - full/extended version.
3) Доброе Утро (Dobroe Utro)
4) Добрый День (Dobriy Den') - May work as a "good morning/evening" as well.
5) Добрый Вечер (Dobriy Vecher)

Unofficial greeting / Неффициальное приветствие:
1) Привет (Privet) + Как Дела? (Kak Dela?)
2) Привет (Privet) + Что Нового? (Chto Novogo?)
3) Здорово (Zdorovo) - Something you may say to your close friend/friends (Slang greeting).
